================================================================================
IMPORTANT: THRESHOLD ENTRY FOR "LOWER IS BETTER" METRICS
================================================================================

For "Lower is Better" metrics (like Bug Count, Error Rate, etc.), you must enter
threshold values in DESCENDING order - highest value first, lowest last:

Example for "Bug Count" (Lower is Better):
  - Below threshold:       8  (WORST - most bugs, lowest score 3.0)
  - Meets threshold:       6  (gets score 4.25)
  - Good threshold:        4  (gets score 4.5)
  - Very Good threshold:   2  (gets score 4.75)
  - Exceptional threshold: 0  (BEST - zero bugs, highest score 5.0)

COMMON MISTAKE: Don't enter 0 for "Below" threshold! For "Lower is Better",
the "Below" field should have the HIGHEST number (worst case scenario).
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FINAL SCORE CALCULATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Final Combined Score = (Automatic OKR Score × 60%) + (Director Score × 20%) + (HR Score × 20%)

Example:
  - Automatic OKR Score: 4.5
  - Director Rating: 4 stars (4.8125)
  - HR Grade: C (4.75)

  Final Score = (4.5 × 0.60) + (4.8125 × 0.20) + (4.75 × 0.20)
              = 2.7 + 0.9625 + 0.95
              = 4.6125

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DIRECTOR EVALUATION (20% of Final Score)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Rating Scale: 1-5 Stars
Conversion Formula: numeric = 4.25 + (stars - 1) × 0.1875

  Stars    Numeric Value    Meaning
  -----    -------------    -------
  1 star       4.25         Needs Improvement
  2 stars      4.4375       Below Expectations
  3 stars      4.625        Meets Expectations
  4 stars      4.8125       Exceeds Expectations
  5 stars      5.0          Outstanding

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
HR EVALUATION (20% of Final Score)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Rating Scale: Letter Grades (D, C, B, A)
Note: D is the HIGHEST score, A is the LOWEST (reversed scale)

  Grade    Numeric Value    Meaning
  -----    -------------    -------
  D            5.0          Outstanding Performance (Best)
  C            4.75         Exceeds Expectations
  B            4.5          Meets Expectations
  A            4.25         Needs Improvement (Lowest)

IMPORTANT: Grade D represents the highest performance level.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BUSINESS BLOCK EVALUATION (Informational Only - NOT in Final Score)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Rating Scale: 1-5 Numeric

  Rating    Meaning
  ------    -------
  1         Needs Improvement
  2         Fair
  3         Good
  4         Very Good
  5         Exceptional

Note: Business Block evaluations are displayed separately and do NOT
contribute to the weighted final score calculation.
